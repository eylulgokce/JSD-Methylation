6.1 Jensen-Shannon Divergence for Methylation Analysis

Jensen-Shannon Divergence (JSD) is an information-theoretic measure based on Shannon entropy (Shannon 1948) that quantifies the difference between probability distributions. It has been widely used in genomic sequence analysis (Grosse et al. 2002) and was applied to DNA methylation by (Kartal, Schmid, and Grossniklaus 2020).

We used the method described in (Kartal, Schmid, and Grossniklaus 2020) to compute JSD from methylation counts across samples. This approach estimates JSD using the plug-in estimator, where probabilities are replaced by observed frequencies. Given read counts for methylated and unmethylated states at each genomic position, the JSD is calculated as:

\[ D_i = H\left( \sum_j \pi_{ij} P_{ij} \right) - \sum_j \pi_{ij} H(P_{ij}) \]

where \(P_{ij}\) is the estimated probability distribution for sample \(j\), and \(\pi_{ij}\) is the sample weight based on read coverage:

\[ P_{ij} = \frac{1}{n_{ij}} \cdot (n_{ij1}, n_{ij2}) \]

\[ \pi_{ij} = \frac{n_{ij}}{n_i} \]

with \(n_{ij}\) as the total read count for sample \(j\) at position \(i\), and \(n_i\) as the total coverage across samples.

To implement this, we adapted the source code from (Kartal, Schmid, and Grossniklaus 2020) and made minor modifications to optimize it for our dataset. These changes ensured compatibility with our sample structure while preserving the original methodology. The calculations were performed in R, using custom functions to efficiently compute JSD across genomic positions.
